WhatsApp for Linux & Windows – Installation Guide

You can use WhatsApp on your computer to send messages, documents, images, and videos directly from your desktop.

LINUX DOWNLOAD

You can install WhatsApp on Linux using the Electron desktop wrapper available on Snapcraft.

Download link:
[https://snapcraft.io/whatsapp-electron](https://snapcraft.io/whatsapp-electron)

Linux Installation Steps

1. Open the download link above in your browser.
2. Make sure Snap is installed on your Linux system.
3. Install WhatsApp using the terminal command:
   sudo snap install whatsapp-electron
4. After installation, open the app from your applications menu or run:
   whatsapp-electron

WINDOWS DOWNLOAD

You can install the official WhatsApp Desktop application for Windows or download it directly from the microsoft store

Download link:
[https://www.whatsapp.com/download](https://www.whatsapp.com/download)

Windows Installation Steps

1. Open the download page above.
2. Download the Windows version of WhatsApp Desktop.
3. Run the installer and complete the installation.
4. Launch WhatsApp Desktop after installation.

LINK YOUR WHATSAPP ACCOUNT (Linux & Windows)

1. When the app opens, a QR code will appear on the screen.
2. Open WhatsApp on your phone.
3. Go to Settings → Linked Devices.
4. Tap “Link a Device”.
5. Scan the QR code displayed on your computer.

Your chats will automatically sync to the desktop application.

SENDING VIDEOS

When sharing videos through WhatsApp:

* Select the video file from your computer.
* Enable HD quality before sending to preserve better resolution.
* If you want to avoid compression completely, send the video as a document/file instead of a normal video.

NOTES

* Your phone must stay connected to the internet for proper synchronization.
* The desktop application works similarly to WhatsApp Web and mirrors your mobile account.
* You can send messages, images, videos, documents, and voice notes directly from your computer.
